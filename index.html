01 — The opportunity

Analytics tell you what happened — but not what to do next

Shopify's analytics do a great job of showing historical data — sales trends, traffic sources, top products. But for most merchants, the harder question is knowing which of those numbers actually need their attention today.

Most store owners are juggling a lot at once. They're not digging into dashboards every morning — and when something shifts quietly in the data, it often goes unnoticed until it's already affected revenue.

What merchants say: Many store owners find it hard to know what to look at in their analytics, and have no clear framework for understanding which metrics actually matter for their stage of growth. — Synthesized from community feedback and App Store reviews, 2025.
Observation 01
Data is reactive by default
Reports surface what already happened. There's no native way to get ahead of a slowing conversion rate or a trending product before the moment passes.
Observation 02
Deeper insights require higher plans
Advanced reporting is tied to higher-tier plans, which means newer and smaller merchants — often the ones with the most to gain — have limited access to analytics tools.
Observation 03
No proactive nudges exist today
The dashboard waits to be opened. There's nothing that flags an unusual checkout drop-off or an inventory risk before a merchant goes looking for it themselves.

02 — The feature

Introducing Shopify Foresight

A native AI intelligence layer built directly into Shopify Admin. Instead of a dashboard you pull, it's a feed that pushes. Every morning it surfaces 3–5 prioritized insights with plain-language explanations and one-click actions — no SQL, no third-party app, no analyst required.

  • Proactive push, not passive pullAnomaly detection, trend acceleration, inventory risk, and revenue leakage are all flagged before you notice them. The dashboard finds you.
  • Natural language Q&A on your storeAsk "why did my conversion drop on Thursday?" and get an answer synthesized across sessions, UTM sources, device type, and cart data — in plain English.
  • One-click actions from every insightReorder inventory, A/B test a checkout element, apply a discount — all executable from the insight card without leaving the screen.
  • Network-level benchmarkingAnonymous cross-merchant comps across 2M+ stores. Your return rate vs. your category. Data no third-party app can ever replicate.

If Foresight ships — what happens next?

Triple Whale focuses on attribution depth
Their strength is pixel-level marketing attribution — a genuinely hard problem they've solved well. That focus means they serve performance marketers at scaling brands best, and are less suited to smaller merchants or non-ad growth channels.
Luca AI adds a financial layer
An interesting approach that bundles Shopify analytics with accounting integrations and access to working capital. Powerful for brands that need a unified financial view, though the setup complexity makes it better suited to larger operations.
The opportunity for Foresight
Both tools require setup, expertise, and ongoing cost. Most Shopify merchants haven't adopted either. A native, zero-setup layer that works on day one for every merchant is a meaningfully different kind of product.
A complementary, not competing, approach
Foresight doesn't need to replace the app ecosystem — tools like Triple Whale go much deeper on attribution than any native feature should. The goal is to serve the merchants in the middle who currently get nothing proactive at all.
04 — Why it works

Shopify already has everything it needs to build this

What makes Foresight interesting as a native feature isn't that it does something third-party tools can't — it's that it removes all the friction those tools require. No app install, no configuration, no monthly fee on top of your plan. It just works, because the data is already there.

Shopify's network of millions of stores also creates a unique opportunity for benchmarking — giving merchants context that no standalone analytics app can offer, because only the platform itself can see across everyone.

When merchants have clear, timely signals from their platform, they're better positioned to grow — and merchants who grow tend to stay. Foresight is as much about helping merchants succeed as it is about building a better analytics feature.

How each tool fits a different merchant

Triple Whale
A great fit for brands running significant paid ad spend who need precise attribution across channels. Purpose-built for performance marketers and worth the investment if ads are a core growth driver.
Luca AI
Well-suited to scaling brands that need a unified view across Shopify, accounting tools, and ad platforms — especially ones that want embedded capital access alongside their analytics.
Foresight
Designed for the merchants who haven't adopted a dedicated analytics tool yet — which is most of them. Zero setup, proactive insights, and benchmarks that make the data feel meaningful from day one.

A phased approach to building it

Phase 1 · Q3 2026
Anomaly alerts
Proactive notifications for conversion shifts, inventory risk, and revenue changes. Native to admin, no setup needed, available on all plan tiers.
Phase 2 · Q4 2026
Daily insight feed + Q&A
A prioritized morning briefing and natural language Q&A on store data. One-click actions on key insights.
Phase 3 · Q1 2027
Network benchmarks
Anonymous cross-merchant comparisons by category — context only a platform with Shopify's scale can provide.
05 — Closing thought

Shopify already holds an incredible amount of useful information for every merchant on the platform. Foresight is an idea for making that information a little more accessible, a little more timely, and a lot more actionable — without any extra work from the merchant.
